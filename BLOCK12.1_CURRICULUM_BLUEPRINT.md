## Curriculum Overview

This curriculum establishes the **Education & Certification Layer** for QuantumPoly v2.0. It is designed to bridge the "epistemic gap" between autonomous adaptive systems and human operators. The program moves beyond static compliance training to cultivate **"Reflexive Ethical Competence"**—the ability to understand, audit, and guide self-modifying governance systems.

The curriculum is anchored in the **"Software–Science–Ethics Fusion"** philosophy, treating human competence as a critical, verifiable component of system integrity (The Epistemic Bridge).

## Learning Paths

### Path 1 – Foundations of Adaptive Governance

**Audience Level:** General Stakeholders, Junior Governance Practitioners, Policy Analysts.

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: The Shift to Adaptive Ethics**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Differentiate between static rule-based compliance (Stage VI) and adaptive, reflexive ethics (Stage VIII).
    - Define "Epistemic Drift" and its risks in autonomous systems.
    - Explain the role of the "Epistemic Bridge" in maintaining human oversight.
  - **Description:** An introductory survey of QuantumPoly’s evolution from rigid compliance frameworks to dynamic, self-modifying governance. Learners explore why traditional governance fails in high-speed autonomous environments and how QuantumPoly v2.0 addresses this gap.

- **Module 2: Regulatory Baselines & Standards**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Map QuantumPoly’s architecture to the EU AI Act (2024 & 2026 Revisions) requirements for human oversight.
    - Apply GDPR principles of transparency to federated data systems.
    - Identify the relevance of IEEE 7000 (Ethical System Design) and ISO/IEC 24029-3 (Neural Network Robustness) in daily operations.
  - **Description:** A rigorous grounding in the external legal and ethical landscape. This module connects abstract regulations to concrete system features, ensuring learners understand the "why" behind compliance protocols.

- **Module 3: Introduction to the Ethical Integrity Index (EII)**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Interpret the components of the EII Score.
    - Analyze the relationship between Transparency, Fairness, and Privacy metrics.
    - Navigate the basic dashboard views of the Transparency Ledger.
  - **Description:** A practical guide to the system's primary metric: the EII. Learners are introduced to the dashboard interfaces developed in Block 10, using them to assess the health of a governance node.

### Path 2 – Technical Administration of Ethics Nodes

**Audience Level:** Node Administrators, DevOps Engineers, System Architects.

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: The Federated Accreditation Protocol**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Understand the cryptographic mechanisms behind cross-node credential verification.
    - Implement the Federated Accreditation API [Inference: Implementation details pending specific API docs].
    - Troubleshoot common verification failures in a decentralized network.
  - **Description:** Technical training on the infrastructure that supports the "Web of Trust." Administrators learn how certifications are issued, signed, and verified across institutional boundaries using the Transparency Ledger.

- **Module 2: Managing Simulation-Linked Learning Environments**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Deploy and maintain "sandboxed" governance scenarios.
    - Configure simulation parameters for specific test cases (e.g., "Transparency Regression").
    - Ensure data privacy by utilizing synthetic data sets for training simulations.
  - **Description:** A hands-on operational guide for managing the Governance Learning Hub's backend. This module focuses on the technical setup required to run safe, isolated experiments without impacting the live production ledger.

- **Module 3: Auditing Algorithmic Reflexivity**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Monitor system logs for signs of "Epistemic Drift."
    - Validate the integrity of automated policy updates.
    - Execute manual overrides in cases of critical ethical divergence.
  - **Description:** Advanced technical oversight training. Administrators learn to distinguish between healthy system adaptation and dangerous drift, using tools from the Continuous Integrity suite (Block 9.8).

### Path 3 – Advanced Cognitive Governance & Temporal Ethics

**Audience Level:** Senior Governance Practitioners, Chief Ethics Officers, External Auditors.

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: Temporal Accountability & The Long-Now**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Interpret "Temporal Impact Reports" generated by the Temporal Ethics Kernel.
    - Evaluate trade-offs between short-term efficiency and long-term ethical stability.
    - Apply the "Ethical Decay Function" concept to policy lifecycle management [Inference: Based on Block 12.1 Architecture].
  - **Description:** A specialized masterclass in time-weighted ethics. Learners develop the cognitive tools to assess decisions not just by their immediate output, but by their projected trajectory over 10, 50, and 100 years.

- **Module 2: Complex Scenario Analysis**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Analyze high-complexity failure modes like "Consent Fatigue" and "Feedback Loop Amplification."
    - Use the Transparency Ledger as a "textbook" to deconstruct historical incidents.
    - Formulate remediation strategies for multi-agent ethical conflicts.
  - **Description:** A case-study-based module using real (anonymized) or high-fidelity synthetic data from the Transparency Ledger. It focuses on the "grey areas" of governance where conflicting values must be balanced.

- **Module 3: Reflexive Feedback Loops**
  - **Learning Objectives:**
    - Design and implement human-in-the-loop feedback mechanisms.
    - Critique automated ethical assessments using qualitative human judgment.
    - Contribute to the "Annual Ethical Competence Report" data collection.
  - **Description:** Training on closing the loop between machine execution and human intent. This module empowers practitioners to actively shape the evolution of the system's ethical models.

## Preparation for Certification

The QuantumPoly Certification Program verifies **"Ethical Reasoning Capabilities"** rather than just technical knowledge.

- **Certification Tracks:**
  - **Certified Governance Practitioner:** Requires completion of Path 1 & Path 3. Assessed via scenario-based exams requiring written justification of ethical decisions.
  - **Certified Node Administrator:** Requires completion of Path 1 & Path 2. Assessed via a practical lab exam involving node configuration and drift mitigation.

- **Assessment Method:**
  - **The Rubric v1.0:** Evaluation based on alignment with the EII, clarity of justification, and adherence to the Reflexive Ethics Charter.
  - **Verification:** All certificates are cryptographically signed and recorded on the Federated Accreditation API.
