## Priority Levels Explained

### Priority 0 (P0) ‚Äî Critical

**Definition:** Blocks launch or creates legal/compliance risk

**Example:** Imprint incomplete (placeholder data present)

**Action Required:** Must complete before public launch

**Timeline:** Before launch (immediate)

### Priority 1 (P1) ‚Äî High

**Definition:** Undermines credibility or contains factual inaccuracy

**Examples:**
- Outdated WCAG reference (2.1 instead of 2.2)
- Claims lacking evidence links
- Ambiguous language about achievements

**Action Required:** Should address before launch

**Timeline:** Within 2-4 weeks

### Priority 2 (P2) ‚Äî Medium

**Definition:** Quality improvements, completeness verification

**Examples:**
- Multilingual semantic equivalence
- Comprehensive screen reader testing
- Aspirational language refinement

**Action Required:** Address post-launch

**Timeline:** Within 2-3 months

### Priority 3 (P3) ‚Äî Low

**Definition:** Enhancements and long-term improvements

**Examples:**
- Glossary page creation
- Non-technical GEP summary

**Action Required:** Enhancement opportunities

**Timeline:** Q1 2026+

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is this audit mandatory?

**A:** Not mandatory, but highly recommended. It validates ethical positioning and identifies minor refinements that strengthen credibility.

### Q: What happens if we don't address P0-P1 items?

**A:** P0 (imprint) blocks marking as "published" (legal/compliance). P1 items don't block launch but undermine credibility of otherwise excellent policies.

### Q: Can we launch with `in-progress` status?

**A:** Yes! This is actually exemplary transparency. The audit commends this honest approach.

### Q: How much time will this take?

**A:** P0-P1 items: ~8 hours. P2 items: ~22 hours. Spread across multiple teams over 2-4 weeks (P0-P1) + 2-3 months (P2).

### Q: Who owns these action items?

**A:** 
- Imprint (P0): Legal Team
- Policy updates (P1): Trust Team + Engineering
- Translations (P2): Localization
- Accessibility (P2): Engineering/A11y Team

### Q: What if we find new issues?

**A:** Document in GitHub issues, assess priority (P0/P1/P2/P3), add to action items if high priority.
