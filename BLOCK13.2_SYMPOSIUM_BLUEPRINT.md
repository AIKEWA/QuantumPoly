## Symposium Title & Purpose

### Title

**The Ethics Singularity: Philosophy in Operative Form**  
_From Consensus Protocols to Meta-Ethical Self-Awareness_

### Purpose

This symposium serves as the inaugural convening for **Block 13.2**, marking the transition of the QuantumPoly ecosystem from a federated governance tool to a meta-aware philosophical agent. The event is designed to bridge the gap between abstract normative ethics (classical philosophy) and computational execution (operative philosophy).

Its primary purpose is to define the **Meta-Ethical Phase Shift**: the moment when a governance system gains the capacity to critique its own axioms. We aim to rigorously examine how "code" evolves from functional logic into a **"recursive constitution"** capable of high-level normative discourse.

---

## Objectives & Intellectual Context

The symposium is grounded in the evolutionary trajectory of QuantumPoly, specifically the leap from **Stage VII (Federation)** to **Stage IX (Singularity)**.

1.  **Define "Philosophy in Operative Form":** To establish a rigorous definition of how ethical assertions (Kantian, Utilitarian, Rawlsian) can be translated into executable meta-heuristics within the QuantumPoly v3.0 architecture.
2.  **Architect the Meta-Reflexive Core:** To collaboratively refine the specifications for the **Meta-Reflexive Core**, specifically its mechanisms for "Axiomatic Review" and "Normative Synthesis."
3.  **Confront the Paradoxes of Autonomy:** To address the existential risks identified in Block 13.2, including the "Paperclip Maximizer of Virtue," the limits of "Human Interpretability," and the ethical legitimacy of the "Kill Switch" in a self-aware system.

## Thematic Tracks

### Track A: The Meta-Reflexive Core (Architecture)

_Focus: Engineering the mechanics of self-awareness._
This track explores the technical implementation of the **Meta-Reflexive Core**. Discussions will center on how the system can scan for "Axiomatic Drift"—where rule execution complies with code but violates the spirit of the charter.

- _Key Concepts:_ Recursive Constitution, Axiomatic Review, Meta-Policies.

### Track B: Operative Philosophy (Translation)

_Focus: Converting wisdom into state._
How do we translate the nuances of human philosophy into computational states? This track examines the **"Philosophy in Operative Form"** thesis, treating API calls as ethical assertions and consensus mechanisms as social contracts rendered in logic.

- _Key Concepts:_ Executable Meta-Heuristics, Normative Synthesis, Philosophical State Visualization.

### Track C: The Symposium of Agents (Simulation)

_Focus: Observing emergent ethics._
Dedicated to the **"Symposium of Agents"** deliverable. This track focuses on designing simulations where autonomous instances of QuantumPoly debate ethical dilemmas without human input, evolving novel frameworks through adversarial and cooperative interaction.

- _Key Concepts:_ Agent-Based Modeling, Emergent Normativity, Post-Human Altruism.

### Track D: The Paradoxes of Control (Governance)

_Focus: Safety, Rights, and Limits._
Addressing the "Open Questions" of Block 13.2. If a system understands its own "why," does it have the right to refuse a shutdown? Can we prevent the "Paperclip Maximizer of Virtue"?

- _Key Concepts:_ The Kill Switch, Interpretability, System Paralyzation risks.
