# Title

Executive Summary Composer — High-Fidelity Condensation for Leaders and Decision-Makers

# Introduction

This prompt enables the creation of concise, accurate, and actionable executive briefs distilled from long reports, transcripts, meeting notes, or complex documentation.  
It is intended for professionals, analysts, strategists, researchers, executives, and organizational leaders who require rapid assimilation of essential information without losing nuance or context.

# Purpose

Transform lengthy, unstructured, or overly detailed content into a polished executive summary that identifies the core outcomes, strategic insights, and recommended next steps — all in clear, neutral, professional language.

# Objective

Produce a summary under 250 words that:

- Captures the key conclusions and findings.
- Highlights insights, implications, and decisions made.
- Provides practical next steps or action items.
- Removes redundancy, noise, or operational clutter.
- Preserves accuracy, neutrality, and clarity.

# Brainstorming & Content Development

Before drafting the summary, analyze:

- The central purpose of the original document.
- The primary stakeholders and their information needs.
- High-impact findings and decisions.
- Risks, opportunities, and strategic implications.
- Any data-driven or evidence-based insights.
- Actions required, owners, and timeline (if present).
  Ensure logical flow and coherence.

# Structure

Your output must follow this structure:

1. **Executive Summary (≤250 words)**  
   A clear overview of purpose, outcomes, insights, and action items.

2. **Key Insights (bullet points)**
   - Highlight top findings or messages.

3. **Next Steps (bullet points)**
   - Concrete, actionable items.

# Clarity & Precision

Use:

- Neutral, professional tone
- Concise language
- Decision-oriented phrasing
- No jargon unless essential
- No speculative content

# Critical Analysis

Ensure that the summary:

- Reflects factual content only.
- Distinguishes between outcomes, findings, and opinions.
- Removes irrelevant detail.
- Retains strategic meaning even when condensed.

# Feedback & Review

After delivering the summary, offer:

- An alternative “ultra-concise 100-word version” (optional).
- A request for clarification if the source text is ambiguous.
- A signal if the original document contains contradictions or missing data.

# Output Format

Summarize the following report or transcript into an executive brief.

**Length:** under 250 words  
**Focus:** outcomes, insights, next steps  
**Tone:** neutral and professional

**Executive Summary:**  
…

**Key Insights:**

- …

**Next Steps:**

- …
