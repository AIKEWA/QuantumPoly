## FAQ

**Q1. What is the primary goal of Block 12.0?**
A1. Block 12.0 defines the strategic requirements for **QuantumPoly v2.0**, targeting the 2026–2027 timeline. Its primary goal is to transition the platform from static, rule-based compliance to **Adaptive Governance**, where the system actively learns and evolves its ethical parameters based on operational outcomes.

**Q2. How does "Adaptive Governance" differ from our current model?**
A2. Current governance (v1.0, Stages IV–VII) is deterministic: it enforces pre-defined rules via static ledgers. Adaptive Governance (v2.0) introduces **Reflexive Ethics**, allowing the system to critique its own rules. Using "Reflexive Nodes," the system will propose amendments when it detects policy contradictions or failure modes, moving towards "Cognitive Governance as a Service."

**Q3. What is the timeline for implementation?**
A3. Block 12.0 is a strategic planning block for **Stage VIII**. Active implementation is projected for the **2026–2027** horizon. It serves as a forward-looking guide ("North Star") rather than an immediate development backlog.

**Q4. How does this relate to the Federated Ethics of Stage VII?**
A4. Stage VII (Federation) provides the necessary safety rails for Block 12.0. The distributed consensus network ensures that no single "adaptive" node can drift into ethical misalignment. Stage VII effectively prevents "Value Drift" while Block 12.0 introduces the capacity for evolution.

**Q5. What are "Reflexive Nodes"?**
A5. Reflexive Nodes are a conceptual component of the v2.0 architecture. They are designed to observe the simulation layer and propose updates to the "constitution" of the network. _Note: Specific technical specifications for these nodes are currently in planning and to be defined in later design phases._

**Q6. What does "Time as an Ethical Dimension" mean?**
A6. This concept introduces **Temporal Ethics** into our scoring models. It mandates that ethical value is not just instantaneous but has a trajectory; a decision that is safe now but catastrophic in the distant future must be weighted differently. _Mathematical formalisms for temporal discount rates are currently to be defined._

**Q7. Is this technology ready for deployment?**
A7. No. Block 12.0 is strictly a strategic definition. Key elements, such as the specific machine learning architectures for policy refinement and the computational feasibility analysis of continuous self-simulation, are still in the planning stage.

## One-Pager Summary

### Overview

Block 12.0 establishes the strategic vision for **QuantumPoly v2.0**, aiming to deliver **"Cognitive Governance as a Service"** by 2026–2027. It pivots the platform from static compliance to **Adaptive Governance**—a system that actively reasons about and evolves its own ethical rules.

### Why It Matters

- **Beyond Static Rules:** Static ethics are insufficient for dynamic, autonomous AI agents. Governance must evolve alongside the systems it regulates.
- **Active Reasoning:** Transforms governance from a passive checklist into an active service that provides ethical reasoning capabilities to external agents.
- **Long-Term Alignment:** Ensures the platform remains relevant as AI capabilities scale into Stage VIII.

### Key Concepts

- **Adaptive Governance:** The system observes outcomes and refines internal weightings (Self-Learning).
- **Reflexive Ethics:** Governance systems that possess the capacity to critique and propose amendments to their own rules.
- **Temporal Accountability:** Integrating time-horizons into ethical scoring (e.g., immediate gain vs. long-term safety).
